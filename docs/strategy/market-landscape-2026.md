# Market Landscape 2026: Marketplaces in Focus Countries

## 1. European E-commerce Overview

EU-27 e-commerce is growing ~5% real from 2024 onward after inflation-driven stagnation. Internet and e-shopper penetration continues to rise (EU-27 e-shoppers 72% in 2024, 74% forecast 2025).

Cross-border commerce is marketplace-led: ~70% of European cross-border e-commerce turnover is marketplace-driven.

### Country Profiles

| Country | Characterization | Growth 2025-2026 |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| NL | Mature & competitive, high online adoption | ~1.3% (2026, consumption-driven) |
| BE | Mature, very EU-cross-border oriented | ~1.0% (2025) |
| DE | Largest market, marketplace-heavy | Stagnation 2025, ~1.2% rebound 2026 |
| FR | Large & regulated, retail media mature | ~0.7% (2025) |
| ES | Strongest macro growth | ~2.9% (2025) |
| IT | Late majority, growth but slow macro | ~0.4% (2025) |

### NL Market Size

NL consumers spent ~€36 billion online in 2024. The market is large, mature, and volume-rich.

## 3. Macro Trend: Marketplaces vs. Land Economy

In low-growth + price-pressure environments, buying behavior shifts to channels that bundle value + certainty: fast delivery, easy returns, reviews, buyer protection, one-click checkout. This explains why marketplaces in practice often recover faster than standalone webshops.

### Why Marketplaces Win in a Slow Economy

- Consumers seek **deal certainty** (not just cheap, but confident they're getting a good deal)
- **Convenience as new luxury**: search, compare, pay, track, return — all in one place
- **Discovery shifts to marketplaces**: 57% name marketplaces (like Amazon) as primary place to discover new products/brands
- **79%** of consumers believe they find the best deal on marketplaces vs brand sites
- NL consumers are extra deal-sensitive (switch faster on "value for money")

## 5. Buyer Segments Shifting from Webshop to Marketplace

### The "Why" Is Bigger Than Price

**Cognitive certainty (risk reduction)**
Reviews, ratings, buyer protection, familiar checkout, reliable delivery — less "purchase stress." Consumers indicate marketplace preference over brand sites (~2/3 preference in multiple surveys).

**Convenience as new luxury**
"All-in-one": search, compare, pay, track, return.

**Discovery shifts to marketplaces**
Marketplaces become product search engines: 57% name marketplaces as primary place to discover new products/brands.

**Deal certainty**
79% of consumers think they find the best deal on marketplaces vs brand sites.

### Life Stage / Segment

EU data: online buying flow is strongest in 25-34 and 35-44 age groups (highest shares).
