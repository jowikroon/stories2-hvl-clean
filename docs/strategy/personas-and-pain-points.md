# Buyer Personas & Pain Points

## 1. Why There Is Demand (Hard Signals)

- **EU businesses with web sales**: ~45% also use a marketplace (alongside own site/app). Marketplace is mainstream, but far from "well set up."
- **NL businesses**: only 8.7% sold (partly) via an online platform (2022). This points to a large "late majority" that wants to, but gets stuck on knowledge/operations.
- **France**: e-tailers invest priority in IT/cyber, marketing/ads, customer care, logistics — and a portion actively wants to recruit. This is exactly the SaaS playfield (tooling + expertise).
- **SME structural pressure**: OECD describes increasing competition and concentration (platform power), forcing SMEs to professionalize.

## 2. Buyer Personas (Who Buys Fastest?)

### Persona 1: The Manufacturer / Brand Owner

- **Typical companies**: Brands with inventory and margin, little marketplace DNA
- **Trigger**: "We're losing share to resellers / Amazon sellers"
- **Pain points**: Brand registry, content governance, price parity, unauthorized sellers, ads expertise

### Persona 2: The Wholesaler / Distributor

- **Typical companies**: Large catalog, many SKUs, often ERP-first
- **Trigger**: "We have data, but no listing power"
- **Pain points**: Feed quality, variants, EAN/GTIN issues, attributes, inventory sync, returns/RMA

### Persona 3: The Retailer with Stores

- **Typical companies**: NL/BE/DE retail, strong in service but weak digitally
- **Trigger**: "Traffic is declining / store margins under pressure"
- **Pain points**: Operational discipline (SLAs), omnichannel inventory, marketplace pricing, customer service processes

### Persona 4: D2C Brand Under CAC Pressure

- **Typical companies**: D2C brands finding ads expensive
- **Trigger**: "Meta/Google is getting too expensive"
- **Pain points**: Marketplace launch playbook, ads/retail media, margin control, cross-border fulfilment

### Persona 5: Exporter (EU Cross-Border)

- **Typical companies**: NL/BE sellers wanting DE/FR/ES/IT
- **Trigger**: "We want EU scale without 5 local teams"
- **Pain points**: VAT/returns/logistics, local content, pricing per country, compliance & product safety

### Persona 6: Regulated / Liability Categories

- **Typical companies**: Auto, beauty, supplements, electronics
- **Trigger**: "Risk of claims/returns"
- **Pain points**: Compliance, product claims, safety, traceability, content/labeling, return costs

## 3. Where Beginners Get Stuck (Knowledge Gaps)

### A. Launch & Compliance (The "Invisible Blockers")

- VAT/OSS/IOSS basics, invoicing per country, EPR/packaging, product safety, claims/labeling
- Platform policy knowledge: restricted products, brand/IP, safety, DSA-like accountability/risk

### B. Catalog & Content (The "Listing Engine")

- GTIN/EAN hygiene, variant structure, attributes per category/country, image rules, marketplace SEO (title/bullets/A+)
- Catalog governance: who can modify content (especially relevant on platforms where multiple sellers share one listing)

### C. Operations & Performance (What Determines Your "Score")

- Fulfilment choices (FBA/3PL/merchant fulfilled), returns policy, customer service SLA, late shipment rate, cancellations
- Stock sync and "overselling" prevention

### D. Retail Media (Ads) & Profitability

- Campaign structure, budget allocation, TACoS/ACoS vs margin, incrementality, keyword/ASIN targeting
- Profit model per country including fees + return costs + logistics

### E. Data & Integrations (Where Tech Lets You Win)

- ERP/PIM/OMS connections, feed automation, exception handling, monitoring
- GenAI/agentic AI readiness: EU statistics show ~1 in 3 EU individuals used generative AI (2025) — adoption threshold is dropping
